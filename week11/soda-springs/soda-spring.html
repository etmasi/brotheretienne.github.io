Founders of Idea

By Marakavi Etienne

Located in Caribou County of southeastern Idaho, the little town of Soda Springs was an unusual but welcome stop on the Oregon Trail. Travelers enjoyed bathing in the natural mineral springs that bubble out of the earth there. The site became a landmark along the trail and a first destination to travelers in general. This interpretive panel is near the site of Morristown, Idaho, a settlement established by Col. Patrick Connor that now lies under the waters of a reservoir. | Kenneth Mays A state of Idaho interpretive panel near the town notes that Col. Patrick E. Connor (from Fort Douglas, Utah) established a settlement called Morristown, and adjacent army post near the mineral springs in 1863. In subsequent years, the population began to dwindle. In 1870, LDS Church President Brigham Young established what is present-day Soda Springs just east of Connorâ€™s location. In the early 1870s, the Mormon settlers built a cabin to be a summer home for President Young at the site he established. He set up the wide streets and square blocks of the settlement. There are several reservoirs on and near the Caribou-Targhee National Forest that provide excellent angling opportunities. These include Island Park, Ashton, Palisades, Blackfoot, Chesterfield, Daniels, Stone, Alexander, and Oneida Reservoirs. Most of these reservoirs are also excellent places to go to ice fishing. Island Park and Ashton Reservoirs occur on Ashton/Island Park Ranger District. There's a rainbow trout, Yellowstone cutthroat trout, brook trout, kokanee, and whitefish fisheries in Island Park Reservoir and a rainbow trout, brown trout, kokanee, and whitefish fisheries in Ashton Reservoir. Palisades Reservoir occurs on the Palisades Ranger District. It features a Yellowstone cutthroat trout, brown trout, lake trout and kokanee fisheries. Blackfoot Reservoir is downstream of Soda Springs Ranger District on the Blackfoot River. You can catch native Yellowstone cutthroat trout or non-native rainbow trout in this reservoir. Chesterfield, Devil Creek, and Stone Reservoirs are nearest to Westside Ranger District and Curlew Grasslands. Chesterfield features a Yellowstone cutthroat trout and rainbow trout fisheries. Daniels Reservoir provides a native Bonneville cutthroat trout and rainbow trout fisheries with management towards a trophy fisheries. Stone Reservoir, in the Malad River System, provides a rainbow trout, black crappie, largemouth bass, and yellow perch fisheries. Alexander and Oneida Reservoirs occur on the Bear River near Soda Springs and Montpelier Ranger Districts. Montepelier Reservoir occurs on the Montpelier Ranger District where you can catch Bonneville cutthroat trout, rainbow trout, kokanee, or yellow perch. You can catch Bonneville cutthroat trout or yellow perch in Alexander Reservoir and Bonneville cutthroat trout, walleye, yellow perch, and small mouth bass in Oneida Reservoir. Whatever your fishing destination, whenever your season, we have a reservoir for you. Please make sure you check your Idaho Department of Fish and Game or Wyoming Department of Game and Fish regulations prior to heading out.
